The Hunger Games
by Suzanne Collins
Genre: Dystopian
When I first read The Hunger Games, I was completely pulled in by its intensity and pace. The world Suzanne Collins created felt so vivid and chilling—it made me think about power, survival, and resistance in a way I hadn’t before. What really stayed with me was Katniss. I admired her strength, but even more, I connected with her fear, hesitation, and fierce loyalty. I found myself rooting for her not just because she was brave, but because she was human. Every decision she made felt heavy, and I felt that weight as I turned the pages. The book made me uncomfortable at times, but in the best way—it challenged me. I couldn’t stop thinking about the Capitol, the injustice, and how eerily close some parts of that world felt to our own. The Hunger Games wasn’t just a page-turner for me—it was a wake-up call wrapped in a compelling story.
About the Author
Suzanne Collins is the author behind the massively popular Hunger Games trilogy. With a background in television writing and storytelling, she has a talent for crafting fast-paced narratives that also dive deep into big ideas like war, power, and resistance. She started her career writing for children's television before turning to novels, and her experience shows in the vivid worlds she builds and the unforgettable characters she creates. Collins continues to inspire readers of all ages with stories that challenge, entertain, and provoke thought.
